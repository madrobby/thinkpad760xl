Installing the Diskette Drive

(Ref #91.)    

The CD-ROM drive can be removed and replaced with the internal diskette drive supplied with the computer.

To install the diskette drive:

Note:
If you are using DOS or Windows, you can remove the CD-ROM drive without turning off the computer. Refer to the documents supplied with your operating systems.

  1. Close any CD-ROM applications and make sure the CD-ROM tray is closed.

  2. Open the LCD; then open the keyboard.

  3. Lift the handle on the CD-ROM drive with the plastic tape, and remove the CD-ROM drive.


    Attention:
    Make sure that any locking device is unlocked.

    The CD-ROM and diskette drives are very delicate devices that need careful handling. Do not press on, drop, or apply any shock to the drives when they are removed from the computer.

  4. If the diskette drive is installed in the external-diskette-drive case, remove the diskette drive as follows:

    Note:
    For the 760E, 760EL, or 760XL, the external-diskette-drive case (kit) is available as an option.

    1. Remove any diskette from the drive; then disconnect the drive case from the computer, if attached.

    2. While holding the bottom of the case, slide the top cover as shown (), and remove it.

    3. Remove the drive by using the handle as shown ().

    Installing a Secondary Battery Pack in the UltraBay

           

    You can extend battery operation time by using two battery packs installed in the computer at the same time. A secondary battery pack is available as an option. Before installing a secondary battery pack, remove the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive from the UltraBay.

    1. Stop any running applications; then make sure the CD-ROM tray is closed or any diskette is removed from the diskette drive.

    2. Turn off the computer; then disconnect the AC Adapter and all cables from the computer.

      Attention:
      To prevent damage to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive, make sure the AC Adapter is disconnected from the computer.

      The CD-ROM drive and diskette drive are very delicate devices that need careful handling. Do not press on, drop, or apply any shock to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive while it is removed from the computer.

    3. Open the LCD; then open the keyboard.

      Attention:
      Make sure the security latch at the right side of the computer is unlocked. (See step 2.)

    4. Lift the drive handle with the plastic tape, and remove the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive.

    5.  Pinch together both holes and remove the spacer by lifting it up slightly.

    6. Place the spacer to cover the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive connector.

      1. Make sure the projected end fits into the opening .

      2. Place the spacer onto the connector and then open the battery connector cover.

    7. If the following front bezel shown below is attached at the front of the computer, remove the bezel from the inside of the computer.

      1. Slide the lever on the bezel to the right as shown. ()

      2. Pull the upper portion of the bezel () and remove the bezel ().



      Note:
      Keep the diskette drive bezel for future use.

    8.   Install the blank front bezel on the inside of the computer.

      1. Fit notch on the lower portion of the bezel into the catch at the bottom of the drive bezel slot ().

      2. Push the upper portion of the bezel into the slot until it snaps into place ().

      3. Slide the lever on the bezel to the left as shown ().

        The following figure shows the installation as seen from the inside of the computer.  

    9.  Insert the secondary battery pack into the UltraBay.

    10. Close the keyboard and reconnect all cables.
    When Replacing the CD-ROM Drive or Diskette Drive
    Before you replace the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive in the UltraBay:

    1. Cover the battery connector with its cover.

    2. Move the spacer back to its original position (shown in step 5).

    Installing a Secondary Hard Disk Drive in the UltraBay

         

    You can use two hard disk drives at the same time by installing a secondary hard disk in the UltraBay. To use the secondary hard disk, you need to purchase another hard disk drive and a hard disk holder. Before installing the secondary hard disk, remove the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive.

    1. Attach the secondary hard disk drive to the hard disk holder.
      Attention:

      The hard disk drive is a very delicate device that needs careful handling. Do not press on, drop, or apply any shock to the hard disk drive.

      1. Insert the hard disk drive in the hard disk holder so that the slot on the hard disk drive fits into the projection on the hard disk drive.

      2. Press the shaded area until the hard disk firmly snaps into the hard disk holder; then replace the hard disk drive handle .

    2. Close any running applications on the computer; then make sure the CD-ROM tray is closed or no diskette is in the diskette drive.

    3. Turn off the computer; then disconnect the AC Adapter and all cables from the computer.

      Attention:
      To prevent damage to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive, make sure the AC Adapter is disconnected from the computer.

      The CD-ROM drive and diskette drive are very delicate devices that need careful handling. Do not press on, drop, or apply any shock to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive while it is removed from the computer.

    4. Open the LCD; then open the keyboard.

      Attention:
      Make sure the security latch at the right side of the computer is unlocked. (See step 2.)

    5. Remove the battery pack.

    6. Lift the drive handle with the plastic tape, and remove the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive.

    Installing an Option in the UltraBay

    (Ref #92.)                

    The CD-ROM drive or diskette drive and its bezel (on the front of the computer) can be removed to install different options in the UltraBay. Some options may require you to remove the option cover from the left side of the computer.

    Note:
    To install the diskette drive into the UltraBay, see "Installing the Diskette Drive".

    1. Close any running applications; then make sure the CD-ROM tray is closed or any diskette is removed from the diskette drive.

    2. Turn off the computer; then disconnect the AC Adapter and all cables from the computer.

      Attention:
      To prevent damage to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive, make sure the AC Adapter is disconnected from the computer.

      The CD-ROM drive and diskette drive are very delicate devices that need careful handling. Do not press on, drop, or apply any shock to the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive while it is removed from the computer.

    3. Open the LCD; then open the keyboard.
      Attention:
      Make sure the security latch at the right side of the computer is unlocked. (See step 2.)

    4. Remove the battery pack.

    5. Lift the drive handle with the plastic tape, and remove the CD-ROM drive or diskette drive.

    6. If the option you are installing has its own option cover to be attached at the left side of the computer, do the following:

      1. Push the current option cover from the inside of the computer to remove it.

      2. Attach the new option cover that came with your option to the side of the computer.

    7.  Refer to the manual that came with the option to install your option (and front bezel) in the UltraBay.

    8. Replace the battery pack.

    9. Close the keyboard and reconnect all cables.
